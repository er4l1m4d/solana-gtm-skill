# Pitch Deck Outline — VC/Hackathon Pitch Structure

Generate a slide-by-slide pitch structure for Solana projects. Supports two variants: Hackathon Pitch (5-7 slides) and VC Pitch (10-12 slides).

---

## When to Use

Use this when a user says:
- "Help me with a pitch deck"
- "Create a pitch for VCs"
- "Hackathon pitch for Colosseum"
- "How to pitch my Solana project"
- "Investor presentation"

## Execution Mode

First, confirm the user's mode:

**Hackathon Mode (5-7 slides)**
- Focus: Problem → Solution → Demo → Team → Ask
- Timeline: Prepare in 1-2 days
- Skip: Detailed financials, complex market sizing
- Goal: Win the competition

**Founder Mode (10-12 slides)**
- Focus: Full investor narrative with traction
- Timeline: Prepare in 1-2 weeks
- Include: Financials, market size, team bios, ask
- Goal: Raise capital

---

## The Framework: Two Pitch Variants

---

### Variant A: Hackathon Pitch (5-7 Slides)

For Colosseum, Solana Foundation grants, or hackathon competitions.

**Slide 1: Title Slide**
- Project name
- Tagline (1 sentence)
- Team name / logo
- Hackathon name / date

**Slide 2: The Problem**
- What pain point exists?
- Who has this problem?
- How big is this problem?
- Why now?

**Slide 3: The Solution**
- What you built
- How it works (1-2 sentences)
- Key differentiator

**Slide 4: Live Demo**
- Screenshots or video
- Key features walkthrough
- "Try it now" link (if available)

**Slide 5: How It Works (Technical)**
- Architecture diagram
- Solana-specific advantages
- Integration with ecosystem (Jupiter, Metaplex, etc.)

**Slide 6: Traction & Validation**
- Early users / feedback
- Metrics (if available)
- Partnerships / integrations
- Community size

**Slide 7: The Ask & Roadmap**
- What you need (funding, mentorship, partnership)
- What you'll build next
- Timeline

**Hackathon Slide Template:**
```
Slide 1: [Project Name] — [Tagline]
Slide 2: The Problem — [Pain point]
Slide 3: Our Solution — [What you built]
Slide 4: Demo — [Screenshots/video]
Slide 5: How It Works — [Architecture]
Slide 6: Traction — [Metrics/feedback]
Slide 7: The Ask — [What you need]
```

## Slide-by-Slide Guidance

### Slide 2: The Problem

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What's the #1 pain point you're solving?
2. Who experiences this problem? (Be specific)
3. How big is this problem? (Market size)
4. Why hasn't this been solved before?
5. Why is now the right time?

**Example (GMTrade):**
> **Problem:** Trading crypto, FX, and commodities on-chain is expensive (5-45bps fees), slow (order book limitations), and limited (few RWA markets).
>
> **Who:** Retail traders who want CEX-like experience on-chain.
>
> **Market:** $10T+ daily trading volume across crypto, FX, commodities.
>
> **Why Now:** Solana's speed + low fees make RWA trading viable on-chain.

**Do:**
- Be specific (not "DeFi is broken")
- Use numbers (market size, fee savings)
- Make it relatable (who suffers?)

**Don't:**
- Be too broad ("crypto needs better UX")
- Use jargon without explanation
- Skip the "why now"

---

### Slide 3: The Solution

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What's your 1-sentence solution?
2. What's the "magic" moment?
3. What's your key differentiator?
4. How does it work (brief)?

**Example (GMTrade):**
> **Solution:** The On-Chain Robinhood — trade crypto, FX, commodities, and stocks with 0.5bps fees on Solana.
>
> **Magic Moment:** User opens the app, sees gold, oil, EUR/USD alongside BTC/ETH, and trades all with one click.
>
> **Differentiator:** 5-9x cheaper than competitors + RWA support.
>
> **How:** Pooled liquidity model with risk isolation across 90+ markets.

**Do:**
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Make it memorable (tagline, analogy)
- Show, don't tell (demo, screenshot)

**Don't:**
- List 10 features (pick top 3)
- Use technical jargon (keep it simple)
- Be vague ("better trading")

---

### Slide 4: Product Demo

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What's the core user flow?
2. What's the "aha" moment?
3. Can you show it working?

**Example:**
> **Demo Flow:**
> 1. Connect wallet (Phantom/Solflare)
> 2. See dashboard with 90+ markets
> 3. Click "Gold" → see chart + order book
> 4. Place trade → 0.5bps fee, instant execution
> 5. View position → PnL, margin, liquidation price

**Do:**
- Show the product (screenshots, video)
- Keep it simple (3-5 steps)
- Include a "try it now" link if possible

**Don't:**
- Show a wall of text
- Skip the demo (investors want to see it)
- Overwhelm with features

---

### Slide 5: How It Works (Technical)

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What's the architecture?
2. Why Solana?
3. What's your technical moat?
4. How does it integrate with the ecosystem?

**Example (GMTrade):**
> **Architecture:**
> - Pooled liquidity model (like GMX on Ethereum)
> - Risk isolation per market (no cross-contamination)
> - Chainlink Data Streams for real-time pricing
> - Solana's 400ms finality for fast execution
>
> **Why Solana:**
> - 400ms finality (vs 12s on Ethereum)
> - $0.00025 per transaction (vs $5-50 on Ethereum)
> - 65,000 TPS (vs 15 on Ethereum)
>
> **Ecosystem Integration:**
> - Jupiter aggregation for best execution
> - Metaplex for position NFTs
> - Helius for RPC infrastructure

**Do:**
- Show architecture diagram
- Reference Solana's advantages
- Mention ecosystem integrations

**Don't:**
- Get too deep into code
- Skip the "why Solana" (investors care)
- Ignore ecosystem partners

---

### Slide 6: Market Opportunity (VC Pitch)

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What's the TAM? (Total market)
2. What's the SAM? (Your segment)
3. What's the SOM? (Realistic capture)
4. What's the growth rate?

**Example:**
> **TAM:** $10T+ daily trading volume (crypto + FX + commodities)
> **SAM:** $500B daily volume on Solana-based DEXs
> **SOM:** $5B daily volume (1% of SAM in Year 3)
> **Growth:** Solana DeFi TVL growing 50%+ YoY

**Do:**
- Use credible sources (DefiLlama, Solana Foundation data)
- Be realistic (not "we'll capture 50% of crypto")
- Show growth trajectory

**Don't:**
- Make up numbers
- Ignore competition
- Skip the "how you'll capture" this market

---

### Slide 8: Traction & Validation

**Guiding Questions:**
1. What metrics do you have?
2. What's the growth trajectory?
3. What partnerships do you have?
4. What's the community size?

**Example:**
> **Metrics (Month 1):**
> - TVL: $500K
> - Active users: 200
> - Trading volume: $10M
> - Fees generated: $50K
>
> **Partnerships:**
> - Jupiter (aggregation)
> - Raydium (liquidity)
> - Helius (RPC)
>
> **Community:**
> - Discord: 500 members
> - X/Twitter: 2,000 followers

**Do:**
- Show real metrics (even if small)
- Highlight growth rate
- Mention partners and integrations

**Don't:**
- Make up metrics
- Ignore early traction (even if small)
- Skip social proof

---

### Slide 10: Team (VC Pitch)

**Guiding Questions:**
1. Who are the founders?
2. What's their background?
3. Why this team wins?
4. Who are the advisors?

**Example:**
> **Team:**
> - **CEO**: 5+ years DeFi, previously at [Protocol], Stanford CS
> - **CTO**: 10+ years engineering, previously at [Company], MIT EECS
> - **Head of BD**: 5+ years crypto, previously at [Exchange], Wharton MBA
>
> **Advisors:**
> - [Name], Former Solana Foundation
> - [Name], Partner at [VC Firm]
>
> **Why Us:** We've built and shipped DeFi products before. We understand the Solana ecosystem. We have the technical depth to execute.

**Do:**
- Highlight relevant experience
- Show team cohesion
- Mention advisors with credibility

**Don't:**
- List degrees without experience
- Ignore team gaps (address them)
- Skip the "why this team"

---

### Slide 12: The Ask

**Guiding Questions:**
1. How much are you raising?
2. What's the use of funds?
3. What milestones will you hit?
4. What's the timeline?

**Example:**
> **Raising:** $2M seed round
>
> **Use of Funds:**
> - 40% Engineering (team + infrastructure)
> - 30% Marketing (growth + community)
> - 20% Operations (legal, admin)
> - 10% Reserve
>
> **Milestones (12 months):**
> - Month 3: 1,000 active users
> - Month 6: $10M TVL
> - Month 9: Mobile app launch
> - Month 12: $50M TVL
>
> **Timeline:** Close round in 6 weeks

**Do:**
- Be specific (amount, use, milestones)
- Show you've thought through the numbers
- Create urgency (timeline)

**Don't:**
- Be vague ("we need funding")
- Skip the use of funds
- Overpromise (be realistic)

## Presentation Tips

### Storytelling Structure
1. **Hook**: Start with a bold claim or surprising fact
2. **Problem**: Build tension (the pain)
3. **Solution**: Release tension (the fix)
4. **Proof**: Show it works (demo, metrics)
5. **Vision**: Paint the future (market, growth)
6. **Ask**: Be clear (what you need)

### Delivery Tips
- **Practice**: Rehearse 10+ times
- **Timing**: 2-3 minutes per slide (hackathon), 5-7 minutes (VC)
- **Confidence**: Own your numbers, admit unknowns
- **Passion**: Show why you care about this problem
- **Q&A**: Prepare for common questions

### Solana-Specific Pitch Points
- Reference Solana's advantages (speed, fees, ecosystem)
- Mention ecosystem partners (Jupiter, Raydium, Metaplex)
- Use Solana metrics (TPS, active validators, TVL)
- Reference Colosseum / Solana Foundation if applicable
- Highlight Solana community (passionate, engaged)

## Key Principles

1. **Story First**: Investors fund stories, not features
2. **Simplicity**: If you can't explain it in 1 sentence, simplify
3. **Evidence**: Show, don't tell (metrics, demos, proof)
4. **Passion**: Investors bet on founders, not just ideas
5. **Honesty**: Admit unknowns, show learning agility
